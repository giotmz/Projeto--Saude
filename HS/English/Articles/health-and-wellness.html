Find out what care you should take with your skin when exposed to the sun.

The skin is the organ responsible for covering the body and protecting it. Divided in layers, comprises the dermis and epidermis: the dermis is the outer layer of the skin, composed of collagen, elastin, proteins and others that support the epidermis. This, in turn, is the deepest layer of the skin, composed of keratin, melanocytes and immune cells.

As it is the largest organ in the body and responsible for its protection, the skin requires a lot of care. When not taken care of, it can present several problems, such as:
Dryness: caused by lack of natural nutrition, which provides hydration to the skin, and also the non-use of moisturizers cosmetic or pharmaceutical origin.

Burn: caused by excessive exposure to the sun or accidents with hot substances, among others.
Color changes: caused by tanning or the appearance of white, rough, rounded patches that flake irregularly.

As it is the largest organ, the skin must be well cared for to protect the body. against external agents. The use of moisturizers, as well as the intake of foods that are beneficial to the skin, helping to maintain elasticity and softness. You Sunscreens and sunblocks should be used daily, even on sunny days. little sun, as light and some types of lamps radiate onto the skin which can cause premature aging. It is important that the skin is protected against the sun's harmful rays (UVA and UVB) to prevent skin cancer and burns.

If there is a desire to tan, it is suggested that sunscreens ultraviolet are used, as this way the production of melanin will be activated without harm the skin so much.
