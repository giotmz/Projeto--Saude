Health and well-being are two distinct concepts that deserve attention. They are related, among other aspects, to the absence of diseases and satisfaction with life.

Health and well-being is a very complex topic that deserves attention, as it is directly related to the population's quality of life. Ensuring a healthy life and promoting well-being for everyone, at all ages, is the third objective of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) of the 2030 Agenda, signed by member countries of the United Nations (UN).

Health is a difficult concept to define, which varies according to the body, the individual, the place, the time and even the social class of those who conceptualize it. For many, health is just the absence of disease, but, according to the World Health Organization (WHO), health is not only related to physical well-being, but is also related to mental well-being. and social.

Health concept

The best-known concept of health is the one that says that health is the absence of disease. This concept was proposed by Christopher Boorse in 1977 and is still widely used today. However, for the World Health Organization (WHO), health is an even more complex concept. According to the WHO, health can be defined as “the state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of illness”. It is clear, therefore, that for an individual to be healthy, it is not enough to not have diseases, it is necessary to analyze the body, mind and social aspect in which an individual is inserted.

This concept of health is very difficult to find. Furthermore, it is difficult to determine, since it is extremely complicated to admit whether or not an individual has psychosocial well-being, since well-being is a subjective concept and is closely related to the way the individual perceives their life. .

Wellbeing concept

The concept of well-being, like the concept of health, is quite complex and stands out for being a subjective state. According to university professor Vera Maria Ribeiro Nogueira, in her article “Well-being, social well-being or quality of life: the reconstruction of a concept”, some authors consider that the state of well-being is “concerning the conditions of the person to satisfy his material needs and his spiritual aspirations".

In general, well-being is related to objective well-being and subjective well-being. Objective well-being is related to the social and economic aspects of a given individual, while subjective well-being is related to each individual's life experience. Generally, subjective well-being is divided into three categories: satisfaction with life, positive affects and negative affects.

Difference between health and well-being

The term “well-being” has a complex, subjective meaning and is generally used to indicate physical, emotional and social aspects of an individual. It is related to the individual's ability to meet their needs and be satisfied with life. Health, in turn, according to the WHO, refers to well-being. It is considered that, for an individual to be healthy, they must experience the most complete state of physical, mental and social well-being.
