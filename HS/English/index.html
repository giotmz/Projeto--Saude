HEALTH AND WELLNESS

The concept of health and well-being are linked, but not the same

According to the WHO, health can be defined as “the state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not just the absence of illness.” It is clear, therefore, that for an individual to be healthy, it is not enough to not have diseases, it is necessary to analyze the body, the mind and the social aspect in which an individual is inserted

The best-known concept of health is the one that says that health is the absence of disease. This concept was proposed by Christopher Boorse in 1977 and is still widely used today. However, for the World Health Organization (WHO), health is an even more complex concept

The concept of well-being, like the concept of health, is quite complex and stands out for being a subjective state. According to university professor Vera Maria Ribeiro Nogueira, in her article “Well-being, social well-being or quality of life: the reconstruction of a concept”, some authors consider that the welfare state is concerning a person's ability to satisfy their material needs and spiritual aspirations

In general, well-being is related to objective well-being and with subjective well-being. Objective well-being is related to the social and economic aspects of a given individual, while subjective well-being is related to each individual's life experience. Generally, well-being is divided subjective into three categories: satisfaction with life, positive affects and negative affects.

The term “well-being” has a complex, subjective meaning and is generally used to indicate physical, emotional and social aspects of an individual. It is related to the individual's ability to meet their needs and be satisfied with life. Health, in turn, according to the WHO, refers to well-being. It is considered that, for an individual to be healthy, they must experience the most complete state of physical, mental and social well-being.

Therefore, it is vital to consider these various dimensions of well-being when addressing issues related to health and quality of life. Promoting well-being involves not only treating illnesses but also creating environments and policies that facilitate access to healthcare, encourage emotional balance, support healthy social relationships and allow people achieve their personal aspirations, thus contributing to a more complete state of well-being.
